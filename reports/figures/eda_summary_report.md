#### Insight 1: Measurement Methodology Matters
Findex measures "active account ownership" while operators report "registered users". This 10:1 ratio (54M users vs 5.4M active accounts) explains the apparent slowdown.

#### Insight 2: Digital Payments Are Growing Faster Than Accounts
- **P2P Transactions**: Surpassed ATM withdrawals in 2024 (1.08x ratio)
- **Digital Payment Usage**: Estimated 35% (2024) vs 9.45% mobile money accounts
- **Key Finding**: Many use digital payments without formal accounts

#### Insight 3: Event Impacts Have Variable Lag Times
- **Telebirr (2021)**: Full impact visible by 2024 (+15pp account ownership)
- **M-Pesa (2023)**: Early impact visible, full effect expected 2025-2026
- **Fayda Digital ID (2024)**: Infrastructure effect with 18-24 month lag

#### Q1: What drives financial inclusion in Ethiopia?
**Primary Drivers**: 
1. Mobile money platforms (Telebirr, M-Pesa)
2. Digital infrastructure (4G, digital ID)
3. Policy environment (NFIS-II, market liberalization)

**Evidence**: Strong correlation between infrastructure investment and inclusion outcomes.

#### Q2: How do events affect inclusion outcomes?
**Event Impact Pattern**:
- **Product launches**: Immediate user acquisition, gradual behavior change
- **Policy changes**: Slow initial impact, accelerating over 12-36 months
- **Infrastructure**: Enabling effect with 18-24 month adoption lag

#### Q3: Why the 2021-2024 slowdown?
**Three-Part Explanation**:
1. **Measurement gap**: Registered vs active accounts
2. **Adoption lag**: Infrastructure takes time to translate to usage
3. **Structural barriers**: Gender gap and rural access limitations
